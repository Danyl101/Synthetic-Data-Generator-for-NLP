Supreme Industries Q1 Results: Stock falls after earnings miss estimates, decline on all parameters Net profit for the company declined 26% year-on-year to Rs202 crore.Revenue came in marginally lower at Rs2,609 crore, compared to the CNBC-TV18 poll estimate of Rs2,765 crore.24, 2025, 2:29:39 PM IST (Updated) Supreme Industries Ltd. reported its first quarter results during market hours on Thursday, July 24.The numbers came in below CNBC-TV18 poll estimates across all parameters and also declined on a year-on-year basis.Following the announcement, the stock fell to the day's low of Rs4,019.50 However, the stock recovered after the management raised its volume growth outlook for FY26.Supreme Industries now expects volume growth of 15-17% for FY26, compared to its earlier estimate of 10-12%.Net profit for the company declined by 26% from last year to Rs202 crore.A CNBC-TV18 poll expected the company to report a profit of Rs240 crore.Revenue for the company decreased 1% from last year to Rs2,609 crore.A CNBC-TV18 poll had projected the figure to be Rs2,765 crore.At the operating level, Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation and Amortisation (EBITDA) for the quarter fell by 17.6% year-on-year to Rs319 crore, which is lower than the CNBC-TV18 poll figure of Rs380 crore.EBITDA margin for the quarter fell to 12.2% from 14.7% last year.Margin was also lower as compared to CNBC-TV18 poll of 13.7nnouncement, but have recovered from the lows to trade 0.37% lower at Rs4,118.20.The stock has been an underperformance over the last 12 months, having declined 23%.
