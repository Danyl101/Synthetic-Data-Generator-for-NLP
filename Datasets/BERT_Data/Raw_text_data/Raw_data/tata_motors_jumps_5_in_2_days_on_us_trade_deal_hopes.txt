TIL Creatives Tata Motors shares are up following optimism about global trade deals.These deals could lower tariffs on car exports.Shares of Tata Motors surged for a second consecutive session on Thursday, gaining as much as 2.4% to Rs 706.5 on the BSE, driven by optimism surrounding global trade deals that could ease tariffs on automobile exports.The stock has advanced 4.9% over the past two sessions.The broader Nifty Auto index climbed 0.6% on Thursday, tracking a global uptick in automotive shares after the United States signed a landmark trade agreement with Japan this week.The US-Japan deal slashed tariffs on Japanese car exports to the US from 25% to 15%, triggering a rally in global auto markets and fuelling speculation of further trade pacts.Reports suggest that Washington is now close to finalising a similar agreement with the European Union, which could see tariffs on car imports cut to 15% from the current 27.5%.The reports indicated that the EU may agree to this levy to avoid the 30% tariff hike threatened by US President Donald Trump from August 1.Implications for Tata Motors and JLR For Tata Motors, whose luxury arm Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) operates a key plant in Slovakia, a reduction of U.S. tariffs to 15% could significantly benefit exports.About 33% of JLR's volumes come from the U.S. market.The U.K., which is no longer part of the EU, already enjoys a 10% tariff under its separate US-UK trade arrangement.Shares of Tata Motors have fallen 6.5% so far in 2025.The stock is down 4.6% over the last six months but has gained 4.8% in the
